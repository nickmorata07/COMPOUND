MASTER THE BIG FOUR: CHEST, BACK, LEGS, ARMS

The "big four" workouts target the major muscle groups: chest, back, legs, and arms. Chest exercises like bench presses build upper body strength and definition, while back exercises like deadlifts pull-ups improve posture and spinal health.

Leg workouts, including squats and lunges, enhance lower body strength and mobility, and arm exercises such as curls and dips focus on the biceps, triceps, and forearms. Incorporating all-around arm function. Together, these workouts create a balanced, full-body routine for strength and fitness.

LATS

Pull-Up (Wide Grip)

Pull-ups work the back, shoulders, and arms. Use a wide grip to target the lats more effectively.

Lat Pulldown (Wide Grip)

The lat pulldown is excellent for the lats. Use a wide grip to maximize muscle engagement.

Dumbbell Row (Single Arm)

Dumbbell rows build back thickness. Perform them one arm at a time to focus on each side.

MIDDLE BACK

Barbell Row (Underhand Grip)

Using an underhand grip helps target the lower lats and biceps effectively.

Seated Cable Row (Close Grip)

This targets the middle back muscles. Keep your back straight and pull the handle toward your lower chest.

T-Bar Row

T-Bar rows are excellent for targeting the middle back. Keep your elbows close to your body.

LOWER BACK

Deadlift

Deadlifts target the lower back, glutes, and hamstrings. One of the best for building strength.

Superman Exercise

The Superman exercise targets the lower back. Lie face down and lift your arms and legs simultaneously.

QUADS

Incline Barbell Bench Press

Lie on an incline bench and press the barbell up until your arms are fully extended. Lower the barbell back to the starting position.

Leg Press

The leg press is a compound weight training exercise in which the individual pushes a weight or resistance away from them using their legs.

Leg Curls

is an isolation exercise that targets the hamstring muscles. The exercise involves flexing the lower leg against resistance towards the buttocks.

bulgarian split squat

Set the pulleys to the lowest position and grab the handles. Bring the handles up and together in front of your chest.

bulgarian split squat

is a single-leg squat variation that uses your bodyweight to activate muscle groups throughout your entire body.

Hack Squat

An exercise performed on a machine by using the legs to move weight resting on the shoulders

HAMSTRINGS

RDL Dumbbell

Hold a dumbbell in each hand and lower them towards the ground while keeping your back straight. Return to the starting position.

Lying Leg Curl (Machine)

Lie face down on the leg curl machine and curl your legs up towards your buttocks. Lower them back to the starting position.

Chest Dip (Leaning Forward)

Hold the dip bars and lean forward slightly. Lower your body until your arms are at a 90-degree angle, then push back up.

GLUTES & CALVES

Barbell Hip Thrust

Place your upper back on a bench and roll a barbell over your hips. Thrust your hips up towards the ceiling, then lower them back down.

Standing Calf Raise

Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart. Rise onto your toes and hold for a moment, then lower back down. Repeat.

Bulgarian Split Squat

Stand in front of a bench with one foot placed behind you on the bench.

SHOULDER

Dumbbell Shoulder Press

Hold a dumbbell in each hand at shoulder height with your palms facing forward. Press the weights overhead until your arms are fully extended.

Barbell Shrug

Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and hold a barbell with an overhand grip. Shrug your shoulders as high as you can, then lower them back down.

Bent-Over Dumbbell Reverse Fly

Bend at the waist with your back flat and hold a dumbbell in each hand. Raise the weights out to the sides until your arms are parallel to the floor.

BICEPS & TRICEPS

Dumbbell Curl

Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and hold a dumbbell in each hand. Curl the weights up to shoulder height, then lower them back down.

Hammer Curl

Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and hold a dumbbell in each hand with your palms facing your body. Curl the weights up to shoulder height, then lower them back down.

Preacher Curl

Sit at a preacher curl bench with your upper arms resting on the pad. Hold a barbell or dumbbell with an underhand grip.

FOREARM FLEXORS & EXTENSORS

Seated Dumbbell Wrist Curl

Sit on a bench, leaving enough room to place your arms on. Bend forward slightly. Grasp the dumbbell with an underhand grip.

Plate Pinch

Hold a weight plate in each hand with your fingers and thumb. Squeeze the plates as hard as you can, then release.

Wrist Roller

Hold a wrist roller (a bar with a weight attached to a rope) with both hands.
